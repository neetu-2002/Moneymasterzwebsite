PSD3: What is new and how it impacts payments? Embracing Change and Optimizing Efficiency

Payments
July 10, 2023

The financial landscape is no stranger to evolution. The past decade alone has seen unprecedented strides in technological innovation, reshaping how businesses and consumers perceive and interact with banking systems. At the forefront of this revolution are account-to-account payments, an initiative that promises transparency, increased competition, and enhanced innovation. With the advent of PSD3 on the horizon, the dynamics of this financial revolution stand poised for another significant shift.

‍

‍

Introduced by the European Banking Authority (EBA), the third Payment Services Directive (PSD3) is an evolution from its predecessor, PSD2. It is expected to provide clearer definitions of certain terms, such as "payment accounts" and "payment instrument." These definitions will impact the operation of digital wallets, the application of strong customer authentication (SCA), and the way payments are initiated. The EBA also proposes to introduce additional prudential requirements to "significant" and "systemic" payment institutions and electronic money institutions. PSD3 will also clarify the regulation of buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) services, which could have implications for payment service providers (PSPs) that provide BNPL services.

‍

‍

In the digital age, businesses are seeking clarity on PSD3's territorial scope and how it will apply to online services. Furthermore, the proposed definition of “significant” Payment Institutions (PIs) and Electronic Money Institutions (EMIs), which could impact the payments market and the economy overall, is another vital aspect businesses are keeping an eye on.

‍

Despite these upcoming changes, one thing remains constant: the need for businesses to adapt and optimize their operations to stay competitive. In this ever-changing landscape, Contiant emerges as a powerful ally for businesses navigating the uncharted territories of account-to-account payments.

‍

The European Banking Authority (EBA) published an opinion on the review of PSD2 on 23rd June 2022, setting the stage for PSD3. With its primary aim to reduce regulatory arbitrage and ensure technological neutrality, PSD3 has several key propositions that are poised to change the financial landscape.

‍

This is where Contiant comes into the picture.

Trusted by over 2000 banks across 19 countries, Contiant leverages the power of account-to-account payments to provide instant payments, eliminate chargebacks, and lower transaction fees. It offers a seamless and efficient way to transfer funds, significantly reducing processing times and enhancing efficiency.

‍

‍

Contiant: The Best Choice for Account-to-Account Payments

As an innovative platform that leverages the power of Account-to-Account Payments, Contiant offers instant payments, eliminates chargebacks, and lowers transaction fees. The platform is trusted by over 2000 banks across 19 countries, providing a seamless and efficient way to transfer funds.

‍

Instant Payments and No Chargebacks

Contiant allows businesses to benefit from reduced processing times and improved efficiency. Payments are settled instantly into your bank account, effectively eliminating the traditional waiting times associated with banks. Moreover, the platform eliminates the risk of chargebacks, which are common with credit card transactions. This feature protects businesses from potential fraud and financial losses.

‍

‍

Reduced Costs

Using Contiant is also cost-effective. The platform is three times less expensive than credit cards, offering businesses significant savings in transaction fees. This reduction in cost can then be passed on to customers, making the business more competitive.

‍

Customizable Payment Form

Contiant provides businesses with a fully customizable payment form created to convert and retain customers. This customization extends to the colors, layout, and entire payments journey, ensuring that the form aligns perfectly with the brand identity of the business.

‍

Comprehensive Data Access

Contiant provides real-time account balance verification, access to detailed transaction history, and the ability to verify users’ identities, reducing fraud. It also allows businesses to reconcile and track transactions from initiation to settlement, providing payment data whenever needed. 

‍

‍

Conclusion: A Dynamic Future for Banking

The banking landscape is evolving, shaped by regulatory changes such as PSD3 and the rise of innovative account-to-account payment platforms like Contiant. While these changes bring challenges, they also offer significant opportunities for businesses willing to adapt and innovate. As we move forward, the combination of regulatory evolution and technological innovation will continue to redefine what is possible in the financial sector.
